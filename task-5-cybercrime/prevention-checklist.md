1. Use Strong Passwords
Create passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols
Avoid using names, birthdays, or simple passwords like “123456”
Use different passwords for different accounts
2. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)
Add an extra layer of security to your accounts
Even if your password is stolen, your account stays protected
3. Be Careful with Emails & Links
Do not click on unknown or suspicious links
Check sender email addresses carefully
Avoid downloading attachments from unknown sources
 4. Stay Safe with Online Payments (UPI/Banking)
Never share OTP, PIN, or bank details
Double-check payment requests before sending money
Use trusted apps like Google Pay or PhonePe securely
5. Use Secure Websites
Always check for HTTPS in the website URL
Avoid entering personal data on unsecured or unknown sites
 6.Install Apps Carefully
Download apps only from trusted sources like the Play Store
Check reviews and permissions before installing
7. Protect Personal Information
Don’t share your address, phone number, or college details publicly
Adjust privacy settings on social media
8. Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Work
Don’t do banking or login to important accounts on public Wi-Fi
Use mobile data or a secure network instead
9. Stay Aware of Common Scams
Be cautious of fake job offers, lottery messages, and urgent requests
If it sounds too good to be true, it probably is
10. Report Suspicious Activity
Inform your bank or college authority immediately
Report cybercrime at the official portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
