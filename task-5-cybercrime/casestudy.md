Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick individuals into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trustworthy source.
A typical phishing attack begins with the attacker sending a fake email, message, or link that appears to come from a legitimate organization, such as a bank or a popular online service. The message often creates urgency, for example, claiming that the user’s account will be blocked unless immediate action is taken. The victim is then directed to click on a link, which leads to a fake website that looks identical to the real one. When the user enters their login credentials or financial details, the attacker captures this information. In some cases, the attacker may also request an OTP to complete unauthorized transactions.
Students, elderly people, and individuals who are not very familiar with cybersecurity practices are common targets. Attackers often exploit fear, curiosity, or lack of awareness.
The consequences can be severe. Victims may lose money from their bank accounts, have their personal accounts hacked, or even face identity theft. In addition, recovering lost data or funds can be difficult and time-consuming.
This case highlights the importance of verifying links, avoiding suspicious messages, and never sharing confidential information online without proper authentication.
